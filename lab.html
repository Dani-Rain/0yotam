The MEME Lab


The Media Effects, Misinformation, and Extremism (MEME) lab


Our research lab studies mass media effects with a focuse on misinformation, conspiracy theories and extremist discourss.
We highlight qualitative computational and traditional social-psychological methods, while we embracing inovative and exploratory methods, theories, and ideas, based on what serves the research and our members best.
Our members and our studies are diverse, inovative and multidisciplinary by design (we contain multitudes, as Walt Whitman said), and currently expolers:

  • analysis of online extremism platforms, such as Stormfront and Daily Stormer
  • hashtag activism campaigns on Twitter
  • ways to improve science communications
  • The ways politicians use social media
  • News coverage of COVID19 and social protests, to the public discourse around abortions and vaccines


To name just a few ongoing studies. As always, these collaborations are best when students introduce their own interests and ideas, so our mental and physical doors are always open to new members and suggestions.
